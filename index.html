Internal Proposal · Growth

Rewards, rebuilt as one engine.

This proposal folds Smart Points and the Ambassador Program into a single, season-driven loop and retires the one-off Boost giveaway. One pool that every action feeds, a referral rate that climbs from 15% to 50% of referred trading fees, a pUSD payback that rewards heavy traders directly, ambassadors who join from their X bio with no Google Form, and a public leaderboard so every trader can see where they stand. Points accrue toward the future SmartX token. Below is the full rationale, every mechanic, and a live working front-end the team can wire straight to the API.

TL;DR
  • One season pool. Every trade, referral and task feeds it, split pro-rata every 10 days, then it resets so newcomers always have a fair shot.
  • Referral climbs 15% to 50% of your referrals' trading fees, and you also collect 10% of every point they earn from trading and tasks.
  • A track for traders, not just recruiters. Heavy traders earn a pUSD fee payback, 5 to 30% by 30-day volume, so volume is rewarded even with no referrals.
  • Ambassadors join from their X bio. No Google Form, no spreadsheet, no review queue. Big KOLs skip the ladder and deal with BD directly.
  • Points accrue toward the future SmartX token (this only seeds the expectation that points may convert; no TGE plan, date, or value is stated), with a live leaderboard showing exactly where each trader stands.

Philosophy

Anyone can be an ambassador. Partners are earned.

The ambassador program should be effortless to enter and meaningful to climb. Today it is neither: a Google Form to get in, a spreadsheet to track activity, and no real reason to care. Here is the thinking behind the rebuild.

  • The bio is the badge. Add @SmartXTerminal to your X bio and you are an Ambassador, instantly, on the site. No form, no application, no review queue.
  • Partners are a different level. The door is wide open at the bottom, but the top is exclusive. Partners earn real rev-share, and the large KOLs we court sit above the self-serve ladder entirely.
  • KOLs deal with us directly. A big KOL will not grind steps. They DM the BD team and we set custom Partner terms on the spot. Self-serve for the crowd, white-glove for the few.
  • Give them a reason to care. A rate that climbs 15% to 50%, a public leaderboard, and an instant title give every ambassador something concrete to chase. That selling point is missing today.
  • Kill the Google Form. Collecting and managing ambassadors by hand does not scale. Joining, status, and rate all live on the page.
  • No manual QRT chasing. Ambassadors connect their X once; from their handles an automated check reads who actually quote-retweeted, in bulk. No spreadsheet, no manual follow-up.

01 · Diagnosis

Why the current setup leaks

The pieces exist, but they pull in different directions, so effort scatters and nothing accumulates.

  • Three disconnected programs. Smart Points, Boost, and the Ambassador Program each run on their own logic. None reinforces the others, so a user's activity in one does nothing for the others.
  • Earning is flat, not value-based. Points accrue per action, so a $1 trader and a whale bank the same. The payoff is only "future rewards," with nothing to tell a user they are ahead.
  • No sense of standing. Nothing shows a trader their rank, their share of the pool, or how close the next tier is. There is no reason to push.
  • Applications live off-site. Becoming an Ambassador means finding a Medium post, filling in a Google Form, and waiting for a manual review, while the data that actually qualifies you (referral volume, trading) is already on-chain.
02 · Core model

A season pool, split pro-rata

The foundation is one pool per season, set in advance. Season 01 runs 10 days, Jul 25 to Aug 4, with a 1,000,000 pool. The pool is also a growth lever: 1,000,000 is the default, but a hot window can run a boosted season at 2,000,000 or more. At the end of the season the pool is divided across every participant in proportion to the points they earned: your reward is your points over everyone's, times that season's pool.

Why a set, resetting pool

  • Always a fair shot. A fresh pool every 10 days means new users are never locked out by early farmers who hoarded a head start.
  • Self-balancing. A set pool divided by more participants means smaller shares. It cannot inflate away, and it rewards showing up consistently rather than spiking once.
  • The pool is a lever. 1,000,000 is the default, but we can raise a season's pool (say 2,000,000) to fire up a hot window. This is where the old one-off Boost now lives: no separate event, just a bigger season.
  • Renewable urgency. Every season is a new reason to come back. Retention is built into the clock instead of bolted on.
  • Points carry, the rate re-checks on its own clock. Your points add up for life across every season. The referral rate runs on a slower 30-day clock (not the 10-day pool), so ambassadors are not forced to re-grind every single season.
Points become the future SmartX token. The conversion stays undefined, on purpose.

Every point earned this season carries toward the future SmartX token. We are not putting a value, a date, or a ratio on it, and that is deliberate: it protects the upside, keeps us flexible ahead of the TGE, and starves mercenary farmers of a calculator. What stays concrete is the standing a user can see: their rank, their pool share, their tier. They feel they are building something real without us committing to a figure.

How the pool is split

You earn points, and that season's pool (1,000,000 by default) is split by each person's share of the total points earned:

your season reward = ( your points ÷ everyone's points ) × the season pool

Your points come from three inputs: trading volume, social & task points, and 10% of each direct referral's trading and tasks. Volume counts with diminishing returns (a single whale does not simply dwarf everyone, and a light user who does the tasks still holds a real slice) through a concave power curve, spelled out in the spec below. Even with the formula, your reward needs everyone's live total, which stays private, so it is not a plug-in farming calculator.

Your points only ever rise. The number that moves is your pool share (your points over everyone's), and the pool is a set amount each season (1,000,000 by default) with the 10% referral bonus counted inside your points, never minted on top. Example, illustrative: hold 1% of all points and your slice is about 10,000 from a 1,000,000 pool.

A trade lands right on the 10-day boundary. It counts in the season it settles in (UTC). Season 02 starts the instant Season 01 closes, back to back with no gap.

03 · Points

Earning into the pool

Everything a user does earns into the same season pool. In the product this is the cyan world: points and rewards.

How you earn

  • Trading earns points. Every trade earns into the pool, so the more actively a user trades the more they earn. It counts, but it is not the only thing that does.
  • Tasks and milestones earn points. Daily, weekly, and milestone steps (a trade, a volume threshold, connect X, first deposit, invite friends) award small fixed points, the layer that gives even a light user a stake.
  • Your network earns you points. You collect 10% of every point your referrals earn from trading and tasks, so bringing in active traders keeps paying you all season, not just at sign-up.
Boost lives inside the pool now.

Boost is retired as a separate event. Its energy now lives in the season pool: active trading always earns share, and when we want a hot window we simply raise the pool (say to 2,000,000) instead of running a one-off giveaway.

Do daily and weekly tasks reset when the season does? No. Dailies reset every 24h and weeklies every 7 days, on their own clocks. The season is a separate 10-day cycle for the pool and the referral rate.

How is trading volume measured? Notional volume traded, read on-chain, then passed through the diminishing-returns curve. Default: your volume is the notional of your own fills, counted once (in a matched trade the maker and taker each count their own side). team to confirm

04 · Referral & Ambassador

A rate that climbs with your network

The Ambassador Program stops being a separate silo and becomes the referral rate ladder, the mint world: trading and referral. The rate is a share of the trading fees (a flat 0.5%) a user's referrals generate, and it is not flat: it climbs from 15% to 50% as the user contributes more.

Referrals pay you twice

Beyond the fee rate, a user also collects 10% of every point their referrals earn from trading and tasks as part of their points. The fee share rewards the money a referral trades; the point override rewards the activity they keep putting in, all season, from direct referrals only.

These are not a strict sequence. Each condition adds to the rate and can be completed in any order: a large referrer can hit the 20-referral step for +10% without doing the quote-retweet step first. A user's rate is 15% plus everything they have completed, up to 50%.

No Google Form

Referral counts and volume are already on-chain, so eligibility is verified automatically. A user connects X once; the bio and quote-retweets are then read through Twikit (no paid X API) and re-checked every 30 days, with no form to fill. A user is either in, or shown exactly what is left to qualify.

A fast track for large KOLs

KOLs with a big following will not grind the ladder, and they do not have to. They start at Partner, up to 50% from day one, with terms set directly by the BD team through a DM. Self-serve for the many, white-glove for the few.

Rebate is visible and claimable

The referral card shows Total earned, Available (with a Claim button), Paid, and Referred volume. Earnings accrue daily into Available, and the user claims them to their SmartX balance. The rate is a share of the trading fees on every trade a referral makes, from 15% up to 50%.

When does a referral attach to you? On their first deposit, and it stays with you for life. Self-referrals and circular referrals (A refers B, B refers A) do not count.

What makes a referral "active"? $50 or more in trading volume in the last 30 days. The bar filters empty sign-ups, so a tier only counts referrals who actually trade.

Your rate drops at the next 30-day check. Are past rebates clawed back? No. Rebate is paid at the rate in force at the time of each trade; earned rebate is never reversed.

What currency is the rebate, and is there a minimum? Default: pUSD, the same asset as the VIP payback, credited to the trading balance, with no minimum to accrue. team to confirm

05 · Rewarding traders

A track for whales, not just recruiters

The referral ladder rewards bringing people in. Trading volume already earns points, but a heavy trader who refers no one still sits at 15% with no status and no perk. So we add a second track keyed to trading, paid as a fee payback in pUSD, so the fee itself never has to change.

VIP tiers, by 30-day volume

Keyed to a trader's trailing 30-day volume, so anyone who trades enough qualifies, not just the top of a leaderboard. The payback is a share of the fees they already paid, returned in pUSD to their trading balance, so the cost scales with revenue and can never exceed it.

  • Payback, not a discount. The fee stays a flat 0.5% on every trade; qualifying traders simply get a slice of what they paid back in pUSD. This keeps the fee formula clean and can never stack into a negative fee.
  • A human on the other end. Circle traders ($500k+ over 30 days) get a direct Telegram line to the team, revealed privately inside their account once they qualify, so a whale with an issue reaches a person in minutes and the team can catch and control problems fast. Platinum traders ($200k+) get priority support. Handles are never shown publicly.
  • Anti-farm on the expensive end. Travel and gatherings are gated on genuine volume (net deposits, unique counterparties) plus a manual check, so wash volume cannot buy a trip. Circle is the smallest cohort, so vetting stays light.
  • Status only, never the pool. VIP tier does not touch your points, so it cannot double-dip the 1,000,000.

One fee, split cleanly

Referral rebate, KOL links, and VIP payback all touch the trading fee, which sounds tangled. Paying everything as a payback keeps it simple: the full 0.5% fee is always collected, then paid out as independent shares of that same fee.

full fee in → independent shares out → platform floor stays positive

From every fee: the referrer gets their rebate (15 to 50%), the trader gets their VIP payback (0 to 30% by 30-day volume), and the platform keeps the rest. No multiplying, no nesting, no negative fee. Worst case, 50% referral + 30% VIP = 80% out, so the platform always keeps at least 20%.

KOL discount links, a real selling point

Today a referral only rewards the referrer, so a KOL has nothing to offer their audience. We let referrers at Advocate (25%) or higher cut their audience in: they can issue up to three links, for example plain, 10% off, and 15% off. The discount is delivered the same clean way, as a pUSD payback to the invitee.

The KOL funds it from their own rebate, so the platform pays nothing

A 10% link moves 10 points of the referrer's rate to the invitee. A 25% Advocate who issues a 10% link keeps 15%, the invitee gets 10% of their fees back, and the platform's share does not move. On a $10 fee: KOL $1.50, platform $7.50, invitee $1 back.

  • Self-funding rule. A link can only pay out of the rebate the referrer actually earns on that invitee, capped at their own rate. Self-referrals earn no rebate, so a self or sybil link pays zero, which kills the "discount yourself" exploit at the root.
  • Total payback cap, 35%. A KOL discount (up to 15%) and a VIP payback (up to 30%) can both land on one trader, so their total is capped at 35% of the fee: the discount (referrer-funded) pays in full, then the VIP (platform-funded) fills the rest. The platform floor still holds.
  • Advocate tier and up (25%+). Discount links unlock only for referrers at 25% or higher, so only people with real rate to share can issue them, not every user. BD can also fast-track a KOL straight to Partner.

Can a KOL link drain the platform, or discount yourself? No to both. A link pays only out of the referrer's own rebate on that invitee, capped at their rate. Self and sybil referrals earn no rebate, so their link pays zero.

Walk me through the fee on one trade. $10 fee, referred by a 25% Advocate. If the trader is Circle (30% VIP): referrer $2.50, trader $3.00 back in pUSD, platform $4.50. If instead they came through that Advocate's 15%-off link and are Circle: discount $1.50 + VIP capped to $2.00 (15% + 20% = the 35% cap), so invitee $3.50, referrer $1.00, platform $5.50. Every part is a share of the same $10, never negative.

06 · Standing

A live leaderboard

The whole ladder is public. The default board ranks by this season's trading volume, with points boards for last season and all-time, and a user's own row is highlighted so they can see exactly who is right above them and push.

  • Columns: rank, trader (with a generated pixel profile avatar), tier, volume, points, pool share, and 7-day rank movement.
  • Three boards: this season's trading volume, last season's final points, and all-time lifetime points, so recent form and long-term standing both show.
  • Your neighborhood: the rows immediately around a user are always shown, even when they are far from the top, so progress feels reachable.

Why can a lower-volume trader show more points than a higher-volume one? Points include tasks and referral bonuses, not just volume, and the default board sorts by this season's volume, so the two need not line up. A footnote on the board says so.

07 · Design

The interface

Same dark SmartX terminal, same top navigation. The rewards content is rebuilt around one idea: put the season pool and a user's place in it front and center, and let everything else earn into it.

  • Three-color system. Mint anchors trading and referral, cyan anchors points and rewards, and gold marks the future token. Each world reads apart at a glance.
  • Tabs behave like pages. Referral, Points, and Leaderboard each load as their own screen; Referral is the default. This maps cleanly to real routes for the dev team.
  • Typography. A humanist sans for reading, monospace for data, numbers, and labels, on a true-black ground.
  • Brand. The real SmartX pixel-owl logo and header are preserved throughout.

What does a brand-new or disconnected wallet see? Not connected: a preview with a connect / deposit prompt. Once connected and new: Affiliate at 15%, 0 points, no pool share yet, every task open to start, and the ladder showing exactly what unlocks each step.

08 · Implementation

What the team builds

The mockup is a working static front-end, structured so it maps directly to real components and routes. To make it live, the back-end needs to provide:

  • Task tracking for the daily, weekly, and milestone tasks, with per-user progress.
  • Points accrual and season distribution: score each action (trades, tasks, milestones) into points, credit each referrer 10% of their referrals' points, accumulate per season, and settle the fixed pool pro-rata at season close.
  • Referral verification: referral count and referred trading volume on-chain; the X connection, bio, and quote-retweets read through Twikit (not the paid X API) and re-checked every 30 days, keeping the current tier if a check cannot run.
  • Rate engine: compute a user's referral rate as 15% plus every completed, order-independent condition, applied to referred trading fees, and account rebate as earned / available / paid, accruing daily with a user claim to balance.
  • Leaderboard aggregation across three boards: this season's volume, last season's points, and all-time points, each with 7-day rank movement.
Why this holds up long-term

Seasons reset so the game stays fair, pro-rata keeps it inflation-proof, relative standing drives competition, cumulative tiers reward loyalty, and points that carry into the future token keep the whole thing worth playing without us ever pricing it. Growth compounds instead of leaking.

09 · Spec details

Every rule, pinned for the build

The exact ruleset a developer implements. Anything marked internal is deliberately never shown to users.

Season

  • Length 10 days. Season 01 runs Jul 25 to Aug 4 (UTC). Season 02 starts the instant Season 01 closes, back to back with no gap.
  • Auto-settlement. At the exact 10-day mark the season closes: that season's pool is split by each user's final pool share, and the resulting reward (share × pool) banks into their lifetime total.

Pool & points

  • Pool per season, split by share. A pool each season, 1,000,000 by default and adjustable up for a boosted season, handed out by each user's share of the total points earned, referral bonus included. Never minted per dollar, so it cannot inflate.
  • Points, then share. Points are built from trading volume, social & task points, and 10% of each direct referral's trading and tasks. Then pool share = your points ÷ Σ all points and season reward = pool share × the season pool.
  • The volume curve. Notional enters as a concave power curve, volume points = 2.19 × (effective volume)^0.75, applied to cumulative season volume, not per trade, so splitting one big trade into many earns nothing extra.
  • Coefficients (config-tunable). Curvature α = 0.75, balance C = 2.19, set so $5,000 of volume ties a full task season (about 1,300 points). The curve still tapers, but is tilted to reward heavy traders: a whale earns a lot more than a small trader without simply erasing tasks.

  • Anti-farm, and what effective volume is. Launch (v1): effective volume = raw notional minus obvious self-trades and A-to-B circular trades. Sophisticated quality-weighting (win rate, holding time, maker ratio) is Phase 2. The economics already make wash weak: it runs on cumulative volume (splitting is useless), washing $1,000,000 pays $5,000 in fees for a sublinear point gain, and the pool is fixed so a washer only dilutes their own share. And even public, the formula is not a plug-in calculator, since a reward needs everyone's live total.
  • Tunable at launch. α and C are config values. Before Season 01, run a sensitivity pass over the expected volume distribution (what the top 1% takes, what a task-only user holds) and adjust, no redeploy.
  • Points only rise. A user's point total only ever goes up as they earn; the live pool share % is the number that moves as others earn. What each share is worth settles at season close; the point total itself never falls.
  • Lifetime = banked rewards. At close, each season's reward (share × that season's pool) banks to a permanent lifetime total. Per-season points reset to zero for the next pool; lifetime never resets. Because each season mints exactly its pool, the token basis stays bounded: everyone's lifetime totals sum to the sum of every season's pool.

Fees & rebate

  • Flat 0.5% fee, maker and taker the same.
  • Rebate = 15% to 50% of the 0.5% fee a user's direct referrals pay. It accrues daily into Available, and the user claims it to their SmartX balance. States: Total earned, Available (Claim), Paid, Referred volume.
  • One level only. Both the rebate and the point override apply to direct referrals only, no second level.

The 10% point override

  • A referrer's points include 10% of each direct referral's social + volume, not the referral's own override, so it never compounds down a chain.
  • This 10% is part of your own points, so it lifts your share from inside and never mints pool beyond the 1,000,000. It shows as a separate line from your own social + volume, and counts toward the leaderboard and lifetime total.

Referral rate ladder, re-checked every 30 days

Dual clock: the points pool runs on 10-day seasons, but the referral rate runs on a slower 30-day cycle so ambassadors are not forced to re-grind every 10 days. The rate is still not permanent: every condition is re-evaluated every 30 days against a rolling 30-day window. A referral is active when they have traded $50 or more in the last 30 days; the tiers below count active referrals, not raw signups.

Leaderboard

  • Three boards: this season's trading volume, last season's final points, and all-time lifetime points. Points include the 10% override.
  • Row: rank, trader (deterministic pixel avatar from wallet), tier, volume, points, pool share, 7-day rank movement. A user's own row and immediate neighbours are always shown.

Verification & abuse

  • On-chain, automatic: trades, volume, referral links, and referred volume.
  • X checks run through Twikit, not the paid X API. Bio and quote-retweets are read from the connected handle via Twikit, re-checked on the 30-day cycle. This avoids X API cost, quota, and policy risk. Fail-safe: if a check cannot run (X down or rate-limited), the user keeps their current tier rather than being downgraded, so an outage never wrongly demotes an ambassador.

Points favor genuine activity; wash-like or self-directed patterns are dampened by the internal volume curve and quality signals (win rate, holding time, maker orders are candidate inputs). None of this is spelled out publicly, so it cannot be reduced to a formula and farmed. We do not advertise that any trade counts.
  • The $50-in-30-days bar is the anti-farm floor for referrals, so empty sign-ups never count.

Token

  • Lifetime points convert to the future SmartX token. No date, value, or ratio is defined or shown, by design.
